The reduction in semi-natural grassland misclassification as grassland (from 28\% at Stage~1 to 11\% at Stage~5) is not accompanied by a reciprocal increase in the reverse direction: grassland pixels misclassified as semi-natural grassland remain below 1\% throughout all stages (Stage~1: 0.9\%, Stage~5: 0.4\%), confirming that the improvement reflects genuine class separation rather than over-prediction of the minority class. A consistent pattern holds for settlement, where confusion with forest land decreases in both directions across stages (settlement~$\to$~forest land: 8.9\% to 6.0\%; forest land~$\to$~settlement: 1.8\% to 1.1\%).
