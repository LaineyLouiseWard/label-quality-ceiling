The largest per-class gaps between validation and held-out test IoU are observed for semi-natural grassland (17.9~pp) and cropland (12.4~pp), consistent with the greater semantic ambiguity and landscape variability of these classes across the geographic and seasonal range of the test set. Settlement and forest land exhibit smaller gaps (6.1 and 2.7~pp, respectively), indicating that minority-class improvements are partially retained under distribution shift, while the semi-natural grassland gap reflects the residual sensitivity of this class to appearance variation that cannot be resolved through the training interventions evaluated here.
